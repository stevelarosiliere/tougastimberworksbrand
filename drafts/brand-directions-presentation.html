The Brand Personality

Raw Material, Clean Cut.

The wood is wild. Live edge, natural grain, local timber. The work is precise. Festool track saw, kiln dried to spec, delivered in three weeks. Every piece carries both. That tension is the brand.

Direction A — Trade-offs

Strengths

Distinctive. Bold. Stands apart from every woodworker site in Connecticut. Scales beautifully to signage, truck graphics, patches, and lumber stamps. Feels established from day one.

Consideration

Dark and industrial may feel less approachable for some homeowners. The Fairfield County audience skews toward warmth. This direction works best if your photography is strong enough to pop against a dark background.

Direction B — Trade-offs

Strengths

Warmest of the three. Most approachable for homeowners. The light background makes product photography shine. Feels like a showroom, not a shop. Easier for a general audience to trust immediately.

Consideration

Less distinctive in the category. Many woodworker sites use light backgrounds and serif fonts. Could blend in if the photography is not exceptional. Also less bold for signage and physical applications.

Direction C — Trade-offs

Strengths

Most emotionally resonant. The badge scales perfectly to labels, patches, stamps, and vehicle graphics. The generational story is built into the mark, not just the copy. References your exact brand admiration list: Athletic Brewing, Ford, Duluth.

Consideration

The badge identity requires more design precision to execute well across all touchpoints. Slightly more complex than A or B. The payoff is higher, but so is the craft threshold.
