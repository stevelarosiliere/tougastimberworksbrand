## Brand Essence (One Word)

**Conviction.**

Eric bet on himself. He bought a kiln. He went full time. Everything about this brand comes from that decision: confident, irreversible, built to last. The heritage is the depth. The work is the brand.

---

## Brand Personality (Tension Pair)

**Refined Grit**

Eric's work is sophisticated enough for Fairfield County's most discerning homeowners, but it's born from sawdust, sweat, and a chainsaw mill in a garage. The brand never hides the grit. It just presents it with confidence.

This is not "rustic charm." This is a craftsman who knows exactly what he's doing, and his hands prove it.

## Tagline

**Built by hand. Backed by name.**

Why it works:
- The Tougas name IS the warranty. Eric stands behind every piece because he touched every piece.
- Ford "Built Ford Tough" energy: personal accountability as brand identity.
- Works on a hat, a burn stamp, a truck door, a t-shirt, a website hero, an invoice header.

## Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Background | Dark Brown | #292524 | Primary background, cards, sections |
| Deep Dark | Near Black | #1C1917 | Hero sections, alternating depth, footer |
| Light | Off White | #FAFAF9 | Text on dark backgrounds, light logo variant |
| Primary Accent | Burnt Amber | #B45309 | Badges, highlights, accent borders, hover states |
| Secondary Accent | Forest Green | #365314 | CTAs, buttons, success states, action elements |
| Neutral | Warm Gray | #78716C | Secondary text, captions, metadata, borders |

### Usage Rules
- This is a DARK MODE brand. Dark backgrounds (#292524, #1C1917) are the default, not the exception.
- Off White (#FAFAF9) is the primary text color on dark backgrounds.
- Burnt Amber (#B45309) is the brand's signature accent. Use for badge borders, key highlights, and emphasis. Not decoration.
- Forest Green (#365314) appears in action elements (buttons, CTAs, links). It's the "go" color.
- Warm Gray (#78716C) handles secondary text, captions, and subtle borders.
- Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black (#000000). The palette stays warm and grounded.
- Light applications (print, invoices) reverse to Off White backgrounds with Dark Brown text and accents.

## Typography

### Display / Headlines
- **Font:** Oswald
- **Weight:** 600 (Semibold)
- **Style:** Uppercase, condensed
- **Source:** Google Fonts (free)
- **Usage:** H1, H2, hero text, section headers, badges, stamps, labels, navigation, buttons
- **Why:** Built for badges, stamps, labels. Carries weight at any size. Condensed form fits tight spaces (burn stamps, truck decals, patches). Ford/Duluth energy.

### Body / Reading
- **Font:** Lora
- **Weight:** 400 (Regular), 400 italic
- **Source:** Google Fonts (free)
- **Usage:** Body copy, descriptions, about text, manifesto, longer reading
- **Why:** Serif with warmth. Creates contrast with the condensed display type. Reads well at body sizes. Heritage feel without being old-fashioned.

### System Fallback
- **Display stack:** Impact, "Arial Narrow", sans-serif
- **Body stack:** Georgia, "Times New Roman", serif

### Size Scale

## Brand Mark System

### Primary Badge (Heritage Lockup)
- **Type:** Badge with double-border treatment
- **Structure:** Outer border, gap, inner border. Center: "TOUGAS TIMBERWORKS" in Oswald 600 uppercase. Subtitle: "MILL. DRY. BUILD."
- **Color:** Off White (#FAFAF9) on Dark Brown (#292524) background (primary). Reverse: Dark Brown on Off White for light applications.
- **Usage:** Website header, business card front, shop sign, truck door, t-shirt back, marketing materials
- **Design note:** The double-border gives visual weight and heritage authority. Designed for labels, patches, stamps, vehicle graphics.

### Stacked Badge
- **Layout:** Badge format, square orientation. "TOUGAS" on line one, "TIMBERWORKS" on line two, "MILL. DRY. BUILD." below.
- **Style:** Oswald 600, uppercase, with border treatment
- **Usage:** Square applications: social media posts, rear truck window, packaging, vertical signage, patches

### Monogram Badge
- **Text:** TTW
- **Style:** Oswald 600, letter-spacing: 0.1em, inside a circle or shield badge
- **Usage:** Favicon, social avatar, small applications, app icon, watermark
- **Minimum size:** 24px wide (digital), 0.5" wide (print)

### Burn Stamp
- **Design:** TTW monogram inside a circle, single weight lines. Simplified from the digital badge.
- **Dimensions:** 2" diameter standard, scalable
- **Simplification:** No thin details that would bleed when burned. Heavy stroke weight. The condensed Oswald letterforms burn cleanly.
- **Usage:** Burned into every finished piece, branded into slabs, lumber tags
- **Material note:** Test burn on scrap before production. Adjust temperature for hardwoods vs softwoods.

### Favicon
- **Design:** TTW monogram badge
- **Sizes:** 16x16, 32x32, 180x180 (Apple touch icon)
- **Color:** Off White on Dark Brown (#292524) background

### Social Avatar
- **Design:** TTW monogram badge centered on Dark Brown (#292524) square background, text in Off White (#FAFAF9)
- **Size:** 400x400 master, crops to all platform sizes
- **Usage:** Instagram, Facebook, Google Business, Yelp, any profile picture

### Logo Rules
- Minimum clear space: height of the "T" on all sides
- Never stretch, rotate, outline, or add effects
- Never place on busy photography without a solid or semi-transparent backing
- Primary: Off White (#FAFAF9) badge on Dark Brown (#292524)
- Reverse: Dark Brown (#292524) badge on Off White (#FAFAF9)
- Accent variant: Burnt Amber (#B45309) border details on Dark Brown
- The tagline "Built by hand. Backed by name." may appear below the badge, set in Oswald 600, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.1em, at roughly 40% the size of the main wordmark

19. Countertop installed in a customer's kitchen. Wide shot showing the room.

## Voice and Tone

### Tone Descriptors
Honest. Craftsman-confident. Earthy. Approachable. Knowledgeable without pretentious.

### The Voice Is
- A craftsman talking to a neighbor about his work
- Confident because the work speaks for itself
- Warm but not folksy
- Specific about materials, process, and quality
- Connecticut refined, not Brooklyn hipster

### Voice Examples

**Describing a product:**
Say: "Black walnut countertop, kiln dried, custom fit to your kitchen."
Not: "A bespoke, artisanal countertop handcrafted with love."

**Explaining the process:**
Say: "I source the wood, mill it, dry it, and build your piece. Start to finish."
Not: "We leverage our vertically integrated supply chain to deliver end-to-end solutions."

**Talking about quality:**
Say: "That slab has been in the kiln for three weeks. It's ready."
Not: "Our premium lumber undergoes a meticulous curation and drying process."

**Marketing a piece:**
Say: "Live edge mantle, white oak, 8 feet. $2,200 installed."
Not: "Rustic charm meets modern elegance in this one-of-a-kind masterpiece."

**About the business:**
Say: "I went full time because the work was too good to keep part time."
Not: "Our passion for woodworking drove us to pursue our dreams."

**Responding to an inquiry:**
Say: "Send me your kitchen dimensions and I'll pull some slabs that would work."
Not: "We'd love to schedule a consultation to explore your vision."

### Writing Rules (ALWAYS)
- Lead with craft and quality
- Let the wood and work speak
- Keep it personal but professional
- Use specific wood species names, dimensions, and process details
- Write like Eric talks: direct, knowledgeable, no fluff

### Writing Rules (NEVER)
- Never use dashes (em dashes, en dashes). Commas or periods instead.
- No hipster/artisan cliches ("curated," "bespoke," "artisanal")
- No corporate language ("solutions," "leverage," "synergy")
- No overly rustic/backwoods tone ("howdy," "y'all," folksy idioms)
- No exclamation points in headlines or taglines
- No generic wood/nature puns

### Language to Avoid
"bespoke," "artisanal," "curated," "handcrafted with love," "rustic charm," "one of a kind" (overused), "farm to table" (wrong industry, same energy), "passion project," "labor of love"

### Language to Use
"live edge," "kiln dried," "full cycle," "custom," "Fairfield County," "black walnut," "slab," "board foot," specific species names, specific dimensions, "Monroe, Connecticut"

## Heritage Positioning

Eric is a third-generation woodworker. His grandfather made toys and donated them. His father built a canoe over seven years. Eric took the craft full-time and built a business around it.

**How we use this:**
- The three-generation story is TEXTURE, not headline
- It lives in the About section, the brand story, subtle design elements
- The site hero leads with the work and the craft, not the family
- Heritage provides depth and trust

## Products and Services (for Copy Reference)

### Kiln Drying Service
- $0.15 per board foot per day
- Available to other woodworkers, contractors, and hobbyists
- Differentiator: no one else in the area offers this

### Lumber Sales (Highest Margin)
- Black walnut: $15/bf
- Hard maple, cherry, sycamore: ~$12/bf
- White oak: slightly higher
- All kiln dried, unsurfaced/rough

### Custom Furniture
- Countertops: $1,000 to $8,000 (most popular)
- Tables, mantles, floating shelves, stair treads, beam casings
- Live edge is the specialty
- 50% deposit, 2-3 week lead time, 50% on completion
- Delivers within Connecticut (primarily Fairfield County)

### What He Refuses
- Flooring (too labor-intensive)
- Cabinetry
- Rush jobs (but likes quick-turnaround smaller jobs)
