The Story
"For years doing it as a hobby, you fantasize about your job being in the woodshop. Overnight, I was like, it's not really that much of a fantasy. You can do this if you work hard at it."

Eric Tougas didn't ease into this. He didn't test the waters, run a side hustle, or build a business plan. He looked at twenty years of nights and weekends in the shop, looked at the kiln and the slabs drying in the yard, and made a decision. The hobby became the job. The job became the standard he measures everything against. This is a brand built on that conviction.

02 / 15
Brand Manifesto

The Whole Point

For twenty years, the woodshop was where I went to clear my head. Nights. Weekends. Whenever the real job let me go. My grandfather made toys by hand and gave them away. My father spent seven years building a canoe. Wood was never a business in our family. It was just what we did. Then I found chainsaw milling, invested in a kiln, and something clicked. Not a slow realization. A switch. I looked at the shop, looked at the stack of slabs drying in the yard, and thought: you can do this if you work hard at it. So I did. Overnight, the hobby became the job. And the job became the standard I measure everything against.

Here is what I actually do. I source raw logs from local tree services and private land across Fairfield County and beyond. I mill them on site with a chainsaw mill or haul them back to the shop. Black walnut, white oak, cherry, sycamore, maple. I know what each species wants to do when it dries, how it moves, where it checks. Every board goes into my kiln, where I control the temperature and humidity until the moisture content is exactly where it needs to be. Then I build. Countertops, dining tables, fireplace mantles, floating shelves, stair treads, beam casings. Live edge when the slab calls for it. Clean lines when the space demands it. One person handles the full cycle, log to finished piece, because that is the only way to guarantee what comes out the other end.

Nobody else in this area does all four steps. Source, mill, dry, build. Most shops buy pre-dried lumber from a distributor and start from there. That is fine work, and some of those shops are my clients. They bring their wood to my kiln because they trust the process. Competitors buying kiln time from you is about as honest a reference as it gets. When you control every stage, you catch problems early. A crack that would have become a split. A pocket of moisture that would have warped a tabletop six months after install. I would rather find it in the kiln than have you find it in your kitchen.

What Tougas Timberworks means is simple. You get a piece of furniture built by someone who touched the wood at every step. Someone who can tell you which town the tree came from, how long it dried, and why he chose that particular slab for your table. The name on the business is the name of the person doing the work. That is not a tagline. That is the whole point.

Brand Essence

Conviction.

Eric bet on himself. Not gradually. Overnight. Twenty years of craft compressed into a single decision. That certainty is the foundation of everything Tougas Timberworks communicates. Every slab he mills, every board he dries, every piece he builds carries the weight of someone who chose this and never looked back.
